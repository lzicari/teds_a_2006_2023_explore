Project Overview: Policy Impact on Opioid Treatment Admissions
This project utilizes a Lagged Difference-in-Differences (DiD) framework to evaluate the impact of recreational marijuana laws on opioid-related treatment admissions across the United States from 2010 to 2023.
Leveraging the TEDS-A microdata—a dataset of over 20 million records—the analysis integrates state-level socioeconomic confounders via the Census Bureau’s ACS API and employs Two-Way Fixed Effects (TWFE) modeling to isolate policy effects.
The workflow, developed in R, encompasses the full data lifecycle: from the cleaning and standardization of high-volume public health datasets to the generation of publication-quality event-study visualizations and econometric tables.
